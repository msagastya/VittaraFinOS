## Why 9.6 Is Not Enough

Getting to 9.6 means:
- Voice works, OCR works, empty states gone, colors clean.
- Still: the app is reactive. User opens it, looks at numbers, closes it.
- Top finance apps at 9.8+ are **proactive**. They surface the right thing
  at the right moment without being asked.
- 9.8 means a user says "this app knows me." Not "this app tracks me."

The delta from 9.6 to 9.8 is **proactive intelligence + emotional resonance + depth no competitor has**.

## Batch B — Proactive Intelligence Surface
**This is what takes the app from 8.8 → 9.3 in one shot.**
*The AI engine is fully built. It just isn't visible anywhere.*

### B1. Smart Dashboard Cards (dynamic, data-driven)

Replace static dashboard layout with a priority-ranked card feed.
File: `lib/ui/dashboard/smart_card_feed.dart` (new)

Card types (each a separate widget, shown/hidden by AI readiness):

**AnomalyCard** — shown when `recentAnomalies.isNotEmpty`
```
⚡ Unusual spend detected
Swiggy charged ₹1,840 today — 4x your usual ₹460.
[Review]  [Dismiss]
```

**BudgetWarningCard** — shown when `hasBudgetWarnings`
```
⚠ Dining budget at 87%
At this rate, exhausted by the 18th — 12 days early.
[Adjust budget]
```

**CashflowCard** — shown when `hasCashflowWarning`
```
📉 HDFC may drop below ₹5,000
Projected around the 22nd based on current patterns.
[See forecast]
```

**HabitNudgeCard** — shown when `habitNudges.isNotEmpty`
```
🏃 You're on a 12-day streak
₹340 left in your dining weekly limit.
```

**OpportunityCard** — shown when `topOpportunities.isNotEmpty`
```
💡 ₹28,000 sitting idle
Parking in FD at 7% vs savings at 3.5%
→ ~₹490 extra per quarter.
[Consider an FD]
```

**HabitQuestionCard** — shown when `topHabitOpportunity != null` and
cooldown has passed. Full one-question card inline on dashboard.

**MonthNarrativeCard** — shown when `currentMonthNarrative != null`
```
April at a glance
High-spend month — dining drove 38% of expenses.
Savings rate: 22% ↓ from 31% last month.
```

Rules:
- Max 3 cards visible at once — ranked by urgency
- Each card dismissible with swipe
- Dismissed state persists per card ID in SharedPrefs (7-day cooldown)
- Cards animate in on first appearance

## Batch D — Advanced Transaction Intelligence

### D1. Duplicate Detection on Manual Entry
File: `lib/ui/dashboard/quick_entry_sheet.dart`

Before saving: check if a transaction with same amount + same merchant + same day
already exists. If yes, show inline warning:
"Looks like you may have already logged this. Save anyway?"
Not a blocker — user can proceed.

### D2. Smart Category Suggestions
File: `lib/ui/dashboard/quick_entry_sheet.dart`

When merchant is typed/spoken: check `MerchantNormalizer` + transaction history
for this merchant's most frequent category. Auto-select it silently.
User can override — just pre-fills the field.

### D3. Recurring Template Auto-Suggest
File: `lib/ui/dashboard/quick_entry_sheet.dart`

When a transaction matches a detected `RecurringTransaction` pattern
(same merchant, similar amount, interval roughly due):
Show inline chip: "This looks like your monthly Swiggy order — log as recurring?"

### D4. Split Transaction Support
File: `lib/logic/transaction_model.dart` + Quick Entry

Allow one transaction to be split across multiple categories.
Use case: ₹2,400 BigBasket order — ₹1,800 groceries + ₹600 household items.
UI: "Split" button in Quick Entry → adds rows, amounts must sum to total.
Stored as linked transactions with a `splitGroupId` in metadata.

### D5. Transaction Templates (Repeatable)
File: `lib/logic/recurring_templates_controller.dart` (may already exist)

"Save as template" option on any transaction.
Accessible from Quick Entry FAB as a secondary action: "From template"
Shows list of saved templates — tap to pre-fill all fields.

## Batch E — Deep Financial Insights (No Competitor Has These)

### E1. Spending Velocity Chart
File: `lib/ui/spending_insights_screen.dart`

A live "budget burn rate" for the current month:
- X axis: days of month (1–31)
- Y axis: cumulative spend
- Line 1: actual spend curve (solid)
- Line 2: ideal linear spend (dashed, based on monthly budget)
- Line 3: AI projected trajectory (dotted, from pattern detector)
Shows visually whether the user is front-loading spend or back-loading.

### E2. Category Deep-Dive
File: `lib/ui/spending_insights_screen.dart`

Tap any category in the pie/bar chart → drill-down screen:
- This month vs last 3 month average for that category
- Top merchants in category (ranked by spend)
- Day-of-week heatmap for category spend
- Trend: "Dining spend up 24% this month vs your 3-month average"

### E3. Income vs Expense Waterfall
File: `lib/ui/spending_insights_screen.dart`

Monthly waterfall chart: starts at income, deducts each major category,
ends at savings/deficit. Makes the "where did my money go" question visual.

### E4. Net Worth History Chart
File: `lib/ui/net_worth_page.dart`

Store a monthly net worth snapshot in SharedPrefs (one entry per month-end).
Show as a line chart going back up to 12 months.
"Your net worth has grown ₹1.2L in the last 6 months (+18%)."

### E5. Tax Summary (India-specific)
File: `lib/ui/spending_insights_screen.dart` or new screen

Compute from transaction data:
- Estimated Section 80C investments (ELSS, NPS, FD >5yr, life insurance)
- Estimated HRA-eligible rent (if rent tagged transactions exist)
- Total TDS-visible income
- Disclaimer: "This is an estimate — consult a CA for filing."
This feature alone will make the app go viral among salaried Indians.
No competitor has it. It's a 30-line computation.

File: `lib/logic/transactions_controller.dart`

On load failure: offer to restore from last known good state.

## What 10.0 Looks Like

A user wakes up. Their home screen widget says:
*"April: ₹31K / ₹45K budget · 22% saved · Salary due in 3 days"*

They open the app. The dashboard shows:
*"Dining is at 87% this week — you typically overspend weekends. You have ₹380 left."*

They say (hold FAB): *"Add 340 food from HDFC at Zomato"*
App saves it. Speaks: *"₹340 food expense from HDFC. Dining now at 96% for the week."*

They tap Insights. The app says:
*"This was a lighter month — 12% under your average. The unusual thing: you had zero dining transactions on weekdays, all on weekends. That's new."*

They didn't ask any of that. The app knew.
That is 10.0.
